Mushroom Therapy: Measuring the Efficacy of Psilocybin on Johns Hopkins Palliative Cancer Patients

By Makiko Wholey

Medical research on psilocybin, a psychedelic compound in mushrooms, stopped in the U.S. in the 1960s after rampant criminal recreational use raised concerns about safety and influenced general perception. In recent years there’s been a resurgence in research on psilocybin’s therapeutic potential and the results have been surprisingly favorable. Johns Hopkins completed a study this year on 56 patients with life-threatening cancer and a DSM-IV diagnosis of anxiety and depressive mood symptoms. Cancer patients with these symptoms have been associated with a decreased quality of life and increased suicidality. Patients who are anxious and depressed also often do not adhere to recommended treatment. The outcome of the Johns Hopkins study demonstrated the effectiveness of psilocybin to decrease depression and anxiety and increase the quality of life in patients with life-threatening cancer.
